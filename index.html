INTERMITTENT
FASTING
OPTIONAL TOOL · NOT MAGIC
BELLY FAT FRIENDLY

IF doesn't burn fat by itself — calories still rule. But for belly fat specifically, fasting helps a lot: it lowers insulin, improves insulin sensitivity, and naturally cuts ~300 kcal because you skip a meal. It also makes calorie control easier when you're not constantly thinking about food.

THE METHODS

EAT WINDOW · FAST WINDOW · DIFFICULTY

14:10
BEGINNER · WEEK 1–2
Eat: 10 AM – 8 PM · Fast: 14h
Easiest entry. Just push breakfast back 2 hours. Most people already do this on weekends naturally.
BEST FORAnyone new to fasting. No real hunger. Use this for 1–2 weeks before stepping up.
16:8
SWEET SPOT · MAIN PROTOCOL
Eat: 12 PM – 8 PM · Fast: 16h
The classic LeanGains method. Skip breakfast, eat 2 main meals + 1 snack in 8 hours. Sustainable forever, hits all the fat-loss benefits.
BEST FORYour default protocol. Train fasted, break fast at noon with a big protein meal. Easy to hit 140g protein with 2 meals + 1 snack.
18:6
INTERMEDIATE
Eat: 1 PM – 7 PM · Fast: 18h
Tighter window. More autophagy benefits, slightly stronger fat loss. Harder to hit protein with only 2 meals + small snack.
BEST FOROnce 16:8 feels easy. Drop into this 2–3 days/week, not daily, for vegetarians.
20:4
ADVANCED · CAUTION
Eat: 4 PM – 8 PM · Fast: 20h
"Warrior Diet" style. One large meal + one small. Very hard to hit 140g vegetarian protein in 4 hours without GI distress.
BEST FOROccasional use only. Not as a regular protocol for vegetarians.
5:2

NITHILAN'S CHECKLIST FOR IF

1. Start with 14:10 for the first week. Don't jump to 16:8 immediately.
2. Drink 3+ litres of water daily — fasting requires more water, not less.
3. If you feel lightheaded, dizzy, or shaky → break the fast. Try again tomorrow.
4. Don't fast on heavy training days if you're new — eat normally those days for 2 weeks.

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR — DON'T SKIP THIS

Visit your GP — not the internet, not WhatsApp groups — if any of these apply:

• Problem persists for 4+ weeks despite lifestyle changes
• You've completely lost morning erections (a key diagnostic sign)
• Sudden onset (not gradual)
• Any pain involved
• Family history of heart disease, diabetes, or high blood pressure

Ask the doctor for these tests: total testosterone (morning blood draw — must be before 10 AM), HbA1c (3-month blood sugar average), lipid profile (cholesterol), thyroid panel. These are inexpensive and rule out 90% of medical causes. ED in a 27-year-old can be the first sign of cardiovascular disease — taking it seriously now protects you for the next 50 years. Performance anxiety is a self-fulfilling prophecy. The more you worry about lasting, the less you do. The fix is counterintuitive — stop trying to "perform" and start being present.
